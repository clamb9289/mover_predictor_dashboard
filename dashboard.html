How the Model Works — v2

Overview

Each morning before market open, the predictor runs a series of checks before selecting any stocks. It first checks whether the overall market is healthy enough to trade. If the market passes, it scores the full S&P Composite 1500 (~1,500 large/mid/small-cap stocks) on four weighted signals, then applies an overnight gap-down boost to the top-50 shortlist before selecting the top 3 most likely to move during the upcoming session. Stocks with upcoming earnings are excluded entirely.

Step 1 — Market Regime Check

Before any picks are made, the model checks whether SPY (S&P 500 ETF) is down more than 1.5% from its prior close. If it is, no trades are made that day. Fighting a down market is one of the fastest ways to lose money consistently — sitting out on bad days protects capital. (Recalibrated 2026-07-17 from an untested 0.5% — 438 real candidate outcomes across 44 trading days showed no cliff there; if anything, days that would've been skipped at 0.5% had a higher win rate. Raised to 1.5% as a still-conservative circuit breaker for genuinely extreme days rather than picking a precise number off a thin tail.)

Step 2 — Universe Filter

The full S&P Composite 1500 — the S&P 500 (large-cap) plus the S&P MidCap 400 and S&P SmallCap 600, about 1,500 tickers total — is narrowed down before scoring begins. Stocks must have an average daily volume of at least 1.5 million shares and a price between $15 and $600. This eliminates illiquid names that can spike randomly and are hard to exit cleanly, while still catching mid/small-cap movers that a large-cap-only universe would miss entirely.

Step 3 — Earnings Exclusion

Any stock with an earnings announcement within the next 3 days is automatically excluded. Earnings are a coin flip — a stock is just as likely to drop 10% as gain 10% on an earnings surprise. For a strategy targeting consistent 1–5% daily gains, earnings events are pure unnecessary risk.

Scoring Signals

Accumulation Signal
60%
High volume WITHOUT a big price move. This is the signature of smart money quietly buying before a move. Score = volume ratio ÷ (1 + price move). A stock with 3x normal volume but a flat price scores very high.
Sector Momentum
20%
How well is the stock's sector performing today? Stocks in the top-performing sectors get a bonus. The sector tide lifts individual stocks — picking against a weak sector is an uphill battle.
News Sentiment
0%
Headlines from the last 48 hours are still fetched and shown on each pick for context, but as of the 2026-07-10 weekly Model Review, this signal carries zero scoring weight — it no longer influences which stocks get picked.
Relative Strength
20%
The stock's 5-day return vs its sector's return. A stock outperforming its own sector shows individual demand beyond market tailwinds.

Overnight Gap-Down Boost (added 2026-07-17)

Not a weighted signal like the four above — a flat score bonus applied only to shortlisted candidates whose price has gapped down 5%+ overnight (prior close → today's open). A straight linear blend of the gap into the composite score was tested first and showed no real edge (noise, sometimes worse than doing nothing); the effect turned out to be a deep-tail cliff, not a smooth relationship, so it's applied as a threshold instead — the same style of rule as the opening-move and opening-gap filters below. Calibrated across 250 trading days, isolating specifically the days this boost changes the top-3: the promoted pick beat the pick it replaced by roughly +1.4 percentage points of win rate, consistently on both a training window and a held-out validation window. This only reranks within the existing top-50 shortlist — a separate, wider test confirmed the same gap-down effect does not hold for stocks outside that shortlist, so it's not used to source new candidates, only to promote ones the model already likes.

Trade Execution Rules

🕙
Buy at 9:35am ET — a short buffer past the 9:30am open to avoid the initial volatility spike, without giving up most of the first trading hour.
⛔
Opening-move filter — at buy time, a pick already down 5%+ from today's open is skipped rather than bought (still breaking down, not just normal early noise). Backtesting across our own picks showed win rates hold up fine on shallower drops and only turn bad past this depth (5%+ drops: 40% win rate), so the threshold sits there rather than shallower. A bounce-off-low override was also tested and dropped — it showed no consistent effect across any test.
📈
Opening-gap filter — a pick that's already gapped up 1%+ from the prior close its own signals were scored on is skipped. Traced from a real trade (NEM, 2026-07-14: scored off a $93.10 close, opened at $97.81, faded back to ~$94.80 by afternoon) and confirmed across 612 historical picks — gaps of 1%+ showed a -0.38% average return and 35% win rate vs. +0.47%/51% below that threshold. This catches a different failure mode than the opening-move filter above: a move that already happened before today's open, which none of the scoring signals (based on prior daily bars) could see.
⏱
Monitor every 5 minutes from 9:35am to 3:55pm ET.
🟢
Staircase trailing stop — the floor steps up as the stock climbs. Hit +0.5% → floor locks at +0.375% (recalibrated 2026-07-16: simulated 309 real candidate trades across a chronological train/validation split, testing this tier against several trigger/floor combinations — this one gave a consistent +5pp win-rate improvement on both halves of the data over the prior +1%/+0.5% tier, without hurting average return; a lower 0.25% trigger or a 50%-of-trigger floor each captured only about half that benefit). Hit +3% → +3%. Hit +5% → +5%. Hit +7% → +7%. Hit +10% → +10%. Hit +15% → +15%. Beyond +15%, the floor trails dynamically 2% below the all-time intraday peak. If the stock falls back below the active floor at any 5-min check, it's sold to lock in that gain — the active floor is shown live on each pick's card.
🚀
No upside cap — stocks that keep running are held as long as they stay above their active floor level.
🔴
Hard stop at -3% — position sold immediately to cap losses.
🔔
End-of-day close at 3:55pm ET — all remaining positions sold before market close.
🛡
Daily loss limit: 5% — any open positions are liquidated immediately and the bot pauses for the rest of the day if hit (found 2026-07-15: it used to only pause new trades, leaving already-open positions unmonitored for the rest of the day — fixed to liquidate first).
⛔
Total drawdown shutoff: 15% — any open positions are liquidated immediately, then the bot disables itself permanently until manually re-enabled.
📅
No trades on bearish market days — if SPY is down more than 1.5% from prior close, no positions are opened that day.
📊
Earnings blackout — stocks with earnings in the next 3 days are never picked regardless of their score.

What May Be Added Next

A weekly Model Review (see that tab) now tracks which signals actually correlate with outcomes and backtests alternate weight presets against real historical data, flagging a candidate change if one meaningfully outperforms — reviewed by hand, never applied automatically. An hourly intraday volume/momentum scanner and a pre-market/after-hours price-gap scanner (see the Scanners tab) are also live, but track-only for now — neither feeds into live picks until enough data validates them. Other potential future improvements: options flow as a sentiment signal, and tighter sector concentration limits (no more than 1 pick per sector).
