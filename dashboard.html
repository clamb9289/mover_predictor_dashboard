How the Model Works — v2

Overview

Each morning before market open, the predictor runs a series of checks before selecting any stocks. It first checks whether the overall market is healthy enough to trade. If the market passes, it scores ~500 S&P 500 stocks on four signals and selects the top 3 most likely to move during the upcoming session. Stocks with upcoming earnings are excluded entirely.

Step 1 — Market Regime Check

Before any picks are made, the model checks whether SPY (S&P 500 ETF) is down more than 0.5% from its prior close. If it is, no trades are made that day. Fighting a down market is one of the fastest ways to lose money consistently — sitting out on bad days protects capital.

Step 2 — Universe Filter

The full S&P 500 is narrowed down before scoring begins. Stocks must have an average daily volume of at least 1.5 million shares and a price between $15 and $600. This eliminates illiquid names that can spike randomly and are hard to exit cleanly.

Step 3 — Earnings Exclusion

Any stock with an earnings announcement within the next 3 days is automatically excluded. Earnings are a coin flip — a stock is just as likely to drop 10% as gain 10% on an earnings surprise. For a strategy targeting consistent 1–5% daily gains, earnings events are pure unnecessary risk.

Scoring Signals

Accumulation Signal
60%
High volume WITHOUT a big price move. This is the signature of smart money quietly buying before a move. Score = volume ratio ÷ (1 + price move). A stock with 3x normal volume but a flat price scores very high.
Sector Momentum
20%
How well is the stock's sector performing today? Stocks in the top-performing sectors get a bonus. The sector tide lifts individual stocks — picking against a weak sector is an uphill battle.
News Sentiment
0%
Headlines from the last 48 hours are still fetched and shown on each pick for context, but as of the 2026-07-10 weekly Model Review, this signal carries zero scoring weight — it no longer influences which stocks get picked.
Relative Strength
20%
The stock's 5-day return vs its sector's return. A stock outperforming its own sector shows individual demand beyond market tailwinds.

Trade Execution Rules

🕙
Buy at 9:35am ET — a short buffer past the 9:30am open to avoid the initial volatility spike, without giving up most of the first trading hour.
⏱
Monitor every 5 minutes from 9:35am to 3:55pm ET.
🟢
Staircase trailing stop — the floor steps up as the stock climbs. Hit +3% → floor locks at +3%. Hit +5% → floor steps to +5%. Hit +7% → +7%. Hit +10% → +10%. Hit +15% → +15%. Beyond +15%, the floor trails dynamically 2% below the all-time intraday peak. If the stock falls back below the active floor at any 15-min check, it's sold to lock in that gain.
🚀
No upside cap — stocks that keep running are held as long as they stay above their active floor level.
🔴
Hard stop at -3% — position sold immediately to cap losses.
🔔
End-of-day close at 3:55pm ET — all remaining positions sold before market close.
🛡
Daily loss limit: 5% — bot pauses for the rest of the day if hit.
⛔
Total drawdown shutoff: 15% — bot disables itself permanently until manually re-enabled.
📅
No trades on bearish market days — if SPY is down more than 0.5% from prior close, no positions are opened that day.
📊
Earnings blackout — stocks with earnings in the next 3 days are never picked regardless of their score.

What May Be Added Next

A weekly Model Review (see that tab) now tracks which signals actually correlate with outcomes and backtests alternate weight presets against real historical data, flagging a candidate change if one meaningfully outperforms — reviewed by hand, never applied automatically. Other potential future improvements: pre-market gap analysis, options flow as a sentiment signal, and tighter sector concentration limits (no more than 1 pick per sector).
